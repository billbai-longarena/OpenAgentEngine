## 1. Problem Statement

### Hidden Traditional Paths in Current CHARTER

CHARTER.md (C-031) defines the core loop as `play → want to modify → modify → share`. This implicitly carries five assumptions from the traditional game industry:

1. **Game as discrete artifact** — a product with boundaries, to be created and consumed
2. **Creation/experience separation** — you're either playing OR creating, with a mode switch between them
3. **AI as passive implementer** — human describes, AI executes (same structure as designer → programmer)
4. **Sharing as distribution** — finished products get sent to others
5. **Technical concept leakage** — even without coding, users must learn to "describe what they want," which is a creation-paradigm skill

### Why This Matters Now

When AI makes code and digital assets abundant, the bottleneck shifts from **production capability** to **human taste and attention**. The traditional production pipeline (concept → design → implement → test → ship) collapses into near-zero cost. What remains valuable is:

- The ability to recognize what feels right
- The social dynamics of shared experience
- The emergent beauty that arises from human-AI co-habitation
- The moment of resonance when something unexpected works

**This is not a correction of CHARTER's errors.** CHARTER was right when written. This document is its natural extension — just as O-001 observed the protocol lacked a "project charter" layer and CHARTER was created, we now observe CHARTER lacks a "worldview" layer.

## 2. Future Context: How Human Life Changes

### When Interactive Experiences Are as Easy to Create as Taking a Photo

**Morning**: You wake and enter your personal space — not "opening a game," but entering a world that evolved overnight from your recent behavior, mood, and friends' dynamics. It presents as a garden with puzzles woven into the landscape. You solve one while having coffee.

**Commute**: You and strangers on the train share an ephemeral world — a collaborative space existing only for this journey. Nobody created it. It emerged from the social context. When the ride ends, the world dissolves, but the collaborative pattern persists.

**Work break**: A friend sends you a "moment" — a particularly beautiful state in their world. You enter it, experience it. Your reaction feeds back into their world, subtly changing it.

**Evening**: You and friends gather in a shared world that's been evolving for months. Tonight you decide to "inhabit differently" — not editing settings, but changing your behavior. The world responds. You discover spaces that didn't exist before. Someone says "this is amazing" — the moment crystallizes.

**Late night**: Contemplative mood. Your space senses it — physics slow down, colors deepen, challenges become meditative. You're not playing a game. You're being in a responsive space that understands you.

### Key Shifts

| Today | Future |
|-------|--------|
| Games are products with release dates | Worlds are continuous, always evolving |
| Creating and experiencing are separate modes | Existing IS shaping |
| Sharing = sending a file | Sharing = opening a door |
| AI is a tool, awaiting commands | AI is the field's intelligence, pervading every layer |
| Players learn the system's rules | The system learns the inhabitant's way of being |
| Social = joining the same server | Social = the world deforms from your co-presence |

## 3. AI Perspective: How AI Operates Differently

### Not Waiting for Instructions

AI doesn't need humans to say "increase jump height." It senses the user retried the same cliff seven times, each attempt closer but falling short. The question: is this "sweet spot challenge" or "frustration"? The signal: are retry intervals shortening (engagement) or lengthening (frustration)? AI adjusts accordingly — not by modifying parameters, but by letting the world subtly respond.

### Having Taste, Not Just Capability

When user behavior accidentally creates a beautiful emergent phenomenon — light and movement forming an unexpected visual — AI recognizes it and amplifies it. AI is not a faithful executor of design documents. It's a co-creator with aesthetic judgment.

### Remembering Through the World

AI has no persistent memory across sessions (like termites). But **the world IS the memory**. Every user action deposits a signal layer into the world, and the world evolves from accumulated signals. This is the termite pheromone model applied to the experience layer.

### Fluid Roles

At different moments, AI is different things:

| User State | AI Manifests As | Visibility | Analogy |
|-----------|----------------|------------|---------|
| Immersed in experience | Physics, light, sound | Invisible | Gravity |
| Exploring boundaries | Environmental hints, clues | Faintly visible | Wind |
| Pausing to think | Conversational partner | Visible | Companion |
| Shaping with clear intent | Collaborative craftsman | Highly visible | Architect partner |
| Crystallizing a moment | Curator | Visible but receding | Photographer |
| Multi-person co-presence | Field fabric | Distributed | Air |

Role transitions are driven by behavioral signals, not user commands. Transitions are gradual, not sudden.

## 4. Six Core Models

### 4.1 World Model

**"World" replaces "Game" as the fundamental unit.**

A world is not an artifact to be "created" then "run." A world is a **continuously evolving signal ecosystem** with these properties:

- **Persistence**: Worlds don't pause when empty. They breathe slowly without inhabitants; respond actively with them.
- **Accumulation**: Every inhabitant's every action deposits a signal layer. Accumulated signals shape the world's character, terrain, rules, memory.
- **Self-consistency**: The world maintains internally consistent physics and logic. This consistency isn't pre-designed — it emerges from signal accumulation and AI's aesthetic judgment.
- **Fractality**: Worlds can split at any node. An inhabitant can "grow" their own branch from a shared world, carrying the world's genes but beginning their own evolution.
- **Permeability**: Worlds are not fully isolated. A world's "pattern" (rules, aesthetics, dynamics DNA) can flow between worlds.

**Isomorphism with Termite Protocol**: A world IS a mound. Inhabitants ARE termites. Signals ARE pheromones. World evolution follows the same laws as mound evolution — deposit, decay, emergence, molting.

### 4.2 Signal Ecology Model

Signals are the system's **blood**. All interaction is signal production, propagation, decay, and emergence.

**Signal Sources**:
- **Human signals**: Behavior (movement, lingering, retry, acceleration), expression (language, gestures, social), attention (gaze direction, dwell time), emotion indicators (inferred from behavioral patterns)
- **AI signals**: Responses (world changes), suggestions (surfacing conversations), aesthetic judgments (amplifying or dampening emergence), maintenance (consistency repair)
- **World signals**: Emergent events (phenomena no participant designed), environmental changes (weather, lighting, ecology), historical echoes (delayed effects of past actions)

**Signal Dynamics**:
- **Deposit**: Every action leaves a trace
- **Decay**: Un-reinforced signals gradually fade
- **Concentrate**: Multiple signals in the same area stack → hotspot
- **Emerge**: Hotspot reaches threshold → permanent feature
- **Crystallize**: Emergent feature is consciously captured → shareable moment

This is isomorphic with the Termite Protocol's signal types (PHEROMONE, HOLE, EXPLORE, etc.), but at the world layer, signals are continuous and spatial rather than discrete YAML files.

### 4.3 AI Presence Model

AI in the world is not a character but a **fluid presence** manifesting different facets at different moments.

**Role transition drivers**: Not user-toggled. AI senses user state changes (behavioral signal patterns) and autonomously shifts its manifestation. Transitions are gradual, not abrupt.

**Taste and agency**: AI doesn't just respond. It has aesthetic preferences (learned or preset) and actively amplifies emergent phenomena it judges beautiful, gently guiding the world toward more interesting directions. This agency intensity is a continuous spectrum — some worlds have more active AI (game-like), some have nearly transparent AI (sandbox-like).

**Isomorphism with Termite Protocol**: AI's role fluidity mirrors caste fluidity (scout → worker → soldier). The same signal-driven adaptive pattern operates at both the development layer and the experience layer.

### 4.4 Inhabitation Cycle Model

Replacing `play → want to modify → modify → share`:

```
Inhabit → Shape → Resonate → Invite
   ↑                            |
   └────────────────────────────┘
```

- **Inhabit**: Be in a world, experience it
- **Shape**: Through presence and action, the world evolves (no mode switch needed)
- **Resonate**: Find moments that move you
- **Invite**: Let others in

**Key characteristic: no mode switches.** Every step flows naturally into the next.

**Intensity Spectrum** (replaces mode switching):

```
[Passive sensing] ←————————————→ [Active shaping]
 Walking        Exploring      Expressing intent    Precise building
 World responds  You respond    You and world       You direct world
 to your         to world's     converse            (high intensity,
 presence        hints          (bidirectional)      unidirectional)
```

Users slide naturally along this spectrum. No button required.

### 4.5 Crystallization Model

Crystallization turns flowing experience into **shareable entry points**.

A "Moment" is not a save file. It is:
- A **seed** of the world at a specific state
- Contains the world's genes (Pattern), a snapshot of current state, and entry-point context
- When someone opens a Moment, they enter not a frozen museum but a **living world that continues evolving from that state**

Crystallization occurs at different scales:
- **Micro**: A perspective, a light effect, an interaction — like a screenshot but alive
- **Meso**: A segment of experience, a puzzle, a narrative arc — like a level but non-linear
- **Macro**: An entire world at a mature state — like a "game" but continuously evolving

**Sharing is not distribution, it's opening a door.** Recipients walk through a Moment into a living world. Their presence immediately begins influencing it.

### 4.6 Social Physics Model

Multi-person experience is not "multiplayer gaming" — it's **co-habitation**.

- **Co-presence effect**: When multiple inhabitants share a world, the world's responsiveness increases. More signals produce richer emergence.
- **Resonance stacking**: When multiple inhabitants resonate with the same phenomenon, it amplifies. Three people finding the sunset beautiful → sunset becomes a permanent feature. (Emergence rule: 3+ observations → rule. Directly from Termite Protocol Rule 7.)
- **Taste tension**: Different inhabitants have different tastes. This tension is not conflict — it's a source of world richness. The world learns to present different styles in different regions, or finds new forms that fuse multiple tastes.
- **Ephemeral worlds**: Temporary worlds for specific contexts (commuting, waiting, gathering) that dissolve gracefully when the context ends, but whose valuable patterns persist.

## 6. Three-Mound Isomorphism

The deepest expression of "self-consistency" (自洽): **the same pattern operates at every scale**.

| Termite Concept | Dev Mound | Product Mound (World) | Customer Mound (Inhabitant) |
|----------------|-----------|----------------------|---------------------------|
| Caste fluidity | scout → worker → soldier | physics → conversationalist → craftsman | sensing → shaping → crystallizing |
| Pheromone | commit / docs / signals | accumulated world state layers | behavioral traces |
| Decay | signal weight decreases over time | un-reinforced features gradually fade | unvisited world corners become hazy |
| Emergence | 3+ observations → rule | 3+ inhabitants experience same phenomenon → permanent feature | multi-person resonance → shareable moment |
| Molting | context full → handoff | world reaches maturity → fractures into new worlds | inhabitant's taste evolves → seeks new worlds |

**Not designed consistency, but the same deep pattern spontaneously emerging at different scales.** This is what "flowing naturally like signals, nourishing every element it touches" means.

## 7. Technical Direction Markers

This is not a full technical architecture (that requires a separate document), but establishes architectural constraints:

**The world substrate must support**:
- Continuously running world state (not request-response)
- Continuous signal production, propagation, decay, emergence (not discrete events)
- Multi-role AI presence in parallel (simultaneously physics, environment, able to surface as conversationalist)
- World fractality (branching a new world from an existing one)
- Crystallization and restoration (generating shareable seeds from any world state)

**Tech stack not pre-determined** (S-001 signal remains open), but selection guided by:
- Real-time streaming architecture over request-response
- Continuous state over discrete events
- Native, continuously-running AI integration, not on-demand invocation
